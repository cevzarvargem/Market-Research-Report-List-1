<p><p>Food shelf life testing encompasses two primary market types: real-time and accelerated testing. Real-time testing evaluates how food products behave under standard storage conditions over extended periods, providing insights on actual shelf life. In contrast, accelerated testing simulates aging processes through heightened temperature and humidity, allowing for quicker results. Both methods are essential for understanding food stability, safety, and quality, helping manufacturers meet regulatory requirements and consumer expectations while optimizing product development and distribution strategies.</p></p>
