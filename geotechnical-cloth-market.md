<p><p>The geotechnical cloth market is divided into two primary types: synthetic and natural. Synthetic geotechnical fabrics, made from polymers like polypropylene or polyester, offer enhanced durability, moisture resistance, and stability, making them suitable for projects like erosion control and soil stabilization. Natural geotechnical cloths, often derived from biodegradable materials such as jute or coir, are eco-friendly alternatives used in landscaping and agricultural applications. Both types serve critical roles in environmental protection, construction, and improving soil performances.</p></p>
