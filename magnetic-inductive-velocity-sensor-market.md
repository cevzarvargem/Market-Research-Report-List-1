<p><p>The Magnetic Inductive Velocity Sensor market is divided into two main types: active and passive sensors. Active sensors generate their own magnetic field to detect velocity changes, providing real-time and precise measurements. They are often used in dynamic applications where immediate feedback is essential. In contrast, passive sensors rely on external magnetic sources and are typically simpler and more cost-effective. They detect velocity by measuring changes in the magnetic field created by moving objects, making them suitable for various industrial applications.</p></p>
