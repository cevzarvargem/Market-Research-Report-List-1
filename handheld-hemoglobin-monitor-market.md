<p><ul><li>Intrusive</li><li>Non-invasive</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The handheld hemoglobin monitor market is divided into two key types: intrusive and non-invasive devices. Intrusive monitors require a blood sample, typically using a fingerstick or venipuncture, to measure hemoglobin levels accurately. These devices are often used in clinical settings for precise diagnostics. Non-invasive monitors, on the other hand, utilize optical technology to estimate hemoglobin levels without drawing blood, offering users convenience and quicker results, making them suitable for home use and routine screenings.</p></p>
