<p><p>The embedded fingerprint sensor market is segmented into UART and USB interface types. UART (Universal Asynchronous Receiver-Transmitter) interfaces are commonly used for low-power, compact applications, ensuring efficient data transmission between the sensor and microcontrollers. In contrast, USB (Universal Serial Bus) interfaces facilitate higher data transfer rates and are typically employed in applications requiring easy connectivity to computers or other devices. Both interfaces cater to diverse sectors, including security, access control, and mobile devices, enhancing user authentication solutions.</p></p>
