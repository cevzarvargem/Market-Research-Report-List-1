<p><p>Injectable dermal fillers are categorized into biodegradable and non-biodegradable types based on their composition and longevity. Biodegradable fillers, such as hyaluronic acid and collagen, gradually break down in the body, offering temporary results that typically last several months to a year. Non-biodegradable fillers, like silicone or synthetic polymers, provide long-lasting effects but can carry higher risks of complications and may require surgical removal. The choice between these types depends on patient needs, desired outcomes, and safety considerations.</p></p>
