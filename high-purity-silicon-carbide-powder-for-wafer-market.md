<p><p>The high-purity silicon carbide powder market for wafer production consists of three primary methods: the Self-Heating Synthesis (SHS) method, the Acheson method, and the Chemical Vapor Deposition (CVD) method. The SHS method involves a combustion reaction to produce silicon carbide. The Acheson method uses an electric arc furnace to synthesize silicon carbide from silica and carbon. The CVD method deposits silicon carbide layers from gaseous precursors onto substrates. Each method offers unique advantages in purity, production efficiency, and application suitability.</p></p>
